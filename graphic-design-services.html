6 steps to an effective
graphic design workflow

1. Review the brief

Before you jump onto your favorite graphic design platform, go through the creative brief and make sure you understand what’s expected of you.

It’s also good to go through this document with the client as it helps get everyone on the same page.

2. Conduct research

Do your research on the client. Analyze their existing design assets and see what styles, fonts, palettes, and other elements are used.

This will help you decide what path to take and create assets consistent with the client’s branding style.

3. Brainstorm ideas w/ a moodboard

Once you narrow down the options on your mood board, share it with your client.

It should give them an idea of what you’re planning to do with the design.

4. Make an outline

Now is the time to put down those ideas on paper. Explore every design idea popping up in your brain.

You’ll likely come up with several brilliant sketches worth pursuing.

5. Make tweaks based on feedback

Clients can give tough feedback — sometimes even major tweaks that affect your project’s timeline.

Stay professional and embrace feedback to give the client what they want.

6. Wrap up the project

Implement the edits and send the final files to your client.

Remember, the design should impress your client and their audience — not just you!
